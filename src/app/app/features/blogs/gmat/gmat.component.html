The GMAT is a computer-adaptive test that measures analytical, writing, quantitative, verbal, and reading skills in English. It is designed to predict academic performance in graduate management programs such as MBA.

  • Accepted by 7,000+ MBA programs globally.
  • Focuses on analytical and problem-solving skills.
  • Conducted online and at test centers.

ğŸ“‘ GMAT Test Structure

The GMAT exam is divided into four main sections:

Analytical Writing Assessment

1 essay, 30 minutes. Analyzes critical thinking and communication skills.

Integrated Reasoning

12 questions, 30 minutes. Tests ability to analyze and synthesize data.

Quantitative Reasoning

31 questions, 62 minutes. Focus on problem-solving and data sufficiency.

Verbal Reasoning

36 questions, 65 minutes. Evaluates reading comprehension, critical reasoning, and grammar.
