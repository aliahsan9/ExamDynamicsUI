The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is a standardized test required for admission to graduate and business schools worldwide. It evaluates analytical writing, verbal reasoning, and quantitative reasoning skills, making it essential for students aiming for international higher studies.

  • Conducted by ETS (Educational Testing Service).
  • Accepted by universities worldwide for MS & MBA programs.
  • Available in both computer-based and paper-based formats.

The GRE requires a balanced approach to verbal, quantitative, and analytical sections. Key strategies include:

  • Build a strong vocabulary for the verbal reasoning section.
  • Practice quantitative problems regularly with shortcuts.
  • Improve analytical writing by practicing essay prompts.
  • Take full-length mock tests to improve time management.
  • Use online resources, forums, and study groups for guidance.

ğŸ“ How to Apply for GRE?

GRE registration is quick and online. Follow these steps:

  1. Go to the official ETS GRE website (ets.org/gre).
  2. Create an ETS account and log in.
  3. Select your preferred test date and location.
  4. Fill out personal and academic details.
  5. Pay the exam fee and confirm your registration.
